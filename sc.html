Schemes

Explore the various government schemes available for the citizens. Below are the details for eligibility, application process, and benefits for each scheme.

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

PMAY aims to provide affordable housing for all by 2022. It offers subsidies for housing construction or purchase.

  • Eligibility: Economically weaker sections, low-income groups, and middle-income groups.
  • Benefits: Interest subsidy on home loans, affordable housing units, financial assistance for construction.
  • Application Process: Applications can be submitted online through the official PMAY portal.

Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA)

MGNREGA provides guaranteed employment to rural households to create sustainable assets and infrastructure.

  • Eligibility: Rural households willing to do unskilled manual labor.
  • Benefits: 100 days of guaranteed employment, wages paid for work completed.
  • Application Process: Individuals can apply through Gram Panchayats and local government offices.

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

PMJDY aims to ensure access to financial services, such as banking, savings, and insurance for all households.

  • Eligibility: All individuals, especially those from underprivileged sections of society.
  • Benefits: Zero-balance bank accounts, overdraft facility, accident insurance cover.
  • Application Process: Applicants can visit nearest banks or financial institutions to open accounts.

Maharashtra State Scholarship Scheme

This scheme provides scholarships to economically disadvantaged students for further education in Maharashtra.

  • Eligibility: Students from economically weaker sections of society in Maharashtra.
  • Benefits: Financial assistance for tuition fees, exam fees, and other educational expenses.
  • Application Process: Applications are accepted online through the Maharashtra State Scholarship portal.

Maharashtra Rajiv Gandhi Jeevandayee Arogya Yojana (RGJAY)

RGJAY provides free medical treatment for various serious illnesses for the economically weaker sections in Maharashtra.

  • Eligibility: BPL families and individuals below the poverty line in Maharashtra.
  • Benefits: Free medical treatment for surgeries, hospital stays, and diagnostics for over 900 serious diseases.
  • Application Process: Eligible individuals can apply through empanelled hospitals and health centers in Maharashtra.

Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM)

SBM aims to eliminate open defecation and improve solid waste management across rural and urban India.

  • Eligibility: Rural households, urban areas, schools, and public spaces in India.
  • Benefits: Financial assistance for constructing toilets, improving sanitation facilities, and cleaning initiatives.
  • Application Process: Applications can be made through local municipalities, Gram Panchayats, and Swachh Bharat Mission portals.

Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

APY is a government pension scheme for workers in the unorganized sector, ensuring a monthly pension after retirement.

  • Eligibility: Indian citizens aged between 18 to 40 years working in the unorganized sector.
  • Benefits: Guaranteed pension of ₹1000 to ₹5000 per month after the age of 60, based on contributions.
  • Application Process: Applications can be made through the official APY portal or any authorized bank branch.

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Yojana

This scheme aims to prevent female feticide, promote education for girls, and create a safe environment for them.

  • Eligibility: Parents of girls, especially from disadvantaged sections of society, across India.
  • Benefits: Awareness programs, financial incentives, scholarships for girls' education, and free health services.
  • Application Process: Applications can be made through local authorities, health centers, and schools.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

PMFBY offers insurance coverage to farmers to protect them against crop failures due to natural calamities.

  • Eligibility: All farmers in India, including those who own or lease agricultural land.
  • Benefits: Coverage for crop loss due to droughts, floods, pests, and other natural disasters.
  • Application Process: Farmers can register online or through local agriculture offices and banks.

Maharashtra Vidhwa Pension Yojana

The Maharashtra Vidhwa Pension Yojana provides financial assistance to widows in Maharashtra to improve their living conditions.

  • Eligibility: Widows residing in Maharashtra, aged between 18 to 60 years.
  • Benefits: Monthly pension to assist widows in their living expenses.
  • Application Process: Applications can be submitted to local offices of the Social Welfare Department in Maharashtra.
